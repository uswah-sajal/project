TURKEY

Turkey is a transcontinental country located at the crossroads of Europe and Asia. It is bordered by eight countries and has a diverse landscape that includes stunning beaches, rolling hills, and soaring mountains. Turkey is known for its rich history, beautiful architecture, and delicious cuisine.
The country's most populous city is Istanbul, which straddles the Bosphorus Strait and is home to iconic landmarks such as the Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque, and Grand Bazaar. Beyond Istanbul, Turkey has many other must-visit destinations, such as Cappadocia with its unique rock formations and underground cities, the ancient city of Ephesus with its well-preserved ruins, and the stunning coastal towns of Antalya and Bodrum.
Turkey is also known for its warm hospitality and friendly people, making it a welcoming destination for visitors from all over the world. Whether you're interested in exploring ancient ruins, indulging in delicious cuisine, or soaking up the sun on a beautiful beach, Turkey has something to offer for everyone.

The Blue Mosque, also known as the Sultan Ahmed Mosque, is one of Istanbul's most iconic landmarks and a must-visit destination for tourists. It was built in the early 17th century during the reign of Sultan Ahmed I and is named after its stunning blue tiles that adorn the interior walls. The mosque's six minarets and central dome dominate the skyline of Istanbul's old city and make it a recognizable symbol of the city.

The mosque's interior is breathtaking, with its spacious prayer hall and soaring domes. It is designed in the classic Ottoman style, with intricate calligraphy and floral designs adorning the walls and ceilings. The stained-glass windows and chandeliers add to the mosque's beauty and create a serene and peaceful atmosphere.
The Blue Mosque is still an active place of worship and is open to visitors outside of prayer times. Visitors are required to dress modestly and remove their shoes before entering the mosque. It is also recommended to visit the mosque during non-prayer times to fully appreciate its beauty and avoid the crowds. Overall, the Blue Mosque is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Istanbul and offers a glimpse into the city's rich history and culture..
